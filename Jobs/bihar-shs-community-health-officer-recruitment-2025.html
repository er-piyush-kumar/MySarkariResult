Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the primary responsibility of a Community Health Officer?

CHOs serve as the first point of contact for healthcare services at Health and Wellness Centers. Key responsibilities include:

  • Primary healthcare services
  • Preventive health initiatives
  • Health awareness programs
  • Basic diagnostic services
2. What is the salary structure for Bihar SHS CHO?

The salary for Community Health Officers in Bihar is as per 7th Pay Commission:

  • Basic Pay: ₹25,000/- per month
  • Grade Pay: ₹5,400/-
  • Total In-Hand Salary: Approximately ₹32,000-35,000/month (including allowances)
  • Allowances: DA, HRA, Medical Allowance, Travel Allowance
3. Is there any age relaxation for reserved categories?

Yes, age relaxation is provided as per Bihar government norms:

  • SC/ST: 5 years
  • OBC: 3 years
  • Women: 5 years
  • PH Candidates: 10 years
  • Ex-Servicemen: As per state rules

Age is calculated as on 01/04/2025.

4. What is the selection process for Bihar CHO?

The selection will be through:

  1. Written Examination (200 marks):
    • Community Health (100 marks)
    • Nursing Knowledge (50 marks)
    • General Awareness (50 marks)
  2. Document Verification
  3. Medical Examination

There will be no interview for this recruitment.

5. What documents are required during application?

You need to upload scanned copies of:

  • 10th/12th Marksheet & Certificate
  • B.Sc Nursing/GNM Diploma
  • CCH Certification
  • Photo ID Proof (Aadhar/PAN/Driving License)
  • Caste Certificate (if applicable)
  • Recent passport photo (3.5cm x 4.5cm, white background)
  • Signature (black ink on white paper)
6. Can candidates from other states apply?

No, only candidates who are permanent residents of Bihar can apply. You will need to submit:

  • Domicile Certificate
  • Local Residence Proof

However, candidates who have completed their nursing education from Bihar institutions may apply regardless of native state.

7. What is the probation period after selection?

Selected candidates will undergo:

  • Probation Period: 2 years
  • Training: 6 months intensive training at designated institutes
  • Posting: At Health & Wellness Centers across Bihar

Confirmation will be subject to satisfactory performance during probation.

8. How can I prepare for the CHO exam?

Recommended preparation strategy:

  1. Syllabus Focus:
    • Community Health Nursing
    • Primary Healthcare Concepts
    • National Health Programs
  2. Study Material:
    • NRHM Guidelines
    • Ayushman Bharat Scheme Details
    • Bihar-specific health statistics
  3. Practice: Solve previous years' question papers
9. What are the career growth opportunities?

Career progression path for CHOs:

  • After 5 years: Senior Community Health Officer
  • After 8 years: Block Health Manager
  • After 12 years: District Public Health Officer

Additional qualifications like MPH can accelerate promotions.

10. What COVID precautions are taken during exam?

Exam centers will follow these protocols:

  • Mandatory mask wearing
  • Temperature checks at entry
  • Sanitization stations
  • Social distancing in seating
  • Isolation rooms for symptomatic candidates

Candidates must carry their vaccination certificate or negative RT-PCR report (72 hours prior).
