About South Western Railway Apprenticeship

What is SWR Apprenticeship?

The South Western Railway (SWR) Apprenticeship program is a skill development initiative by Indian Railways to provide technical training to ITI-certified candidates. This program offers hands-on experience in various technical trades, preparing candidates for careers in the railway sector. The apprenticeship lasts for one year, during which candidates receive stipend and practical training.

Significance of SWR Apprenticeship 2025

This recruitment drive for 904 positions is a significant opportunity for ITI graduates across Karnataka and neighboring states. The apprenticeship program offers:

  • Practical experience in railway workshop environments
  • Industry-recognized training certification
  • Potential for future employment opportunities in railways
  • Monthly stipend during training period
  • Exposure to modern railway technologies and practices

Career Insight:

Completing an apprenticeship with Indian Railways significantly enhances your employability in the public and private sectors. Many former apprentices have secured permanent positions in railways and related industries.

Eligibility Criteria

Educational Qualifications

  • Essential: Class 10th (Matriculation) pass with minimum 50% marks
  • Technical Qualification: ITI Certificate in relevant trade from NCVT/SCVT recognized institute
  • Candidate must possess both Matriculation and ITI certificates at the time of application

Physical Standards

For certain trades, candidates must meet specific physical standards:

  • Minimum height: 160 cm for males, 155 cm for females
  • Minimum chest measurement: 81 cm (with 5 cm expansion)
  • Normal vision without color blindness (for technical trades)

Selection Process

Selection Methodology

  1. Merit-Based Selection: Selection will be based on percentage of marks obtained in Matriculation and ITI
  2. Document Verification: Shortlisted candidates will be called for document verification
  3. Medical Examination: Selected candidates must pass the prescribed medical examination

Merit Calculation Formula

Final Merit = (Matriculation percentage × 50%) + (ITI percentage × 50%)

Example: If a candidate scored 75% in Matric and 80% in ITI, the calculation would be:
(75 × 0.50) + (80 × 0.50) = 37.5 + 40 = 77.5%

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Is there any written exam for SWR Apprentice selection?

No, there is no written examination for this recruitment. Selection is purely merit-based, calculated from your Matriculation and ITI marks. Shortlisted candidates will be called directly for document verification.

2. Can candidates from other states apply for SWR Apprenticeship?

Yes, candidates from all states can apply. However, reservation benefits are only applicable to Karnataka domicile candidates. Candidates from other states will be considered under the unreserved category.

3. What is the duration of the apprenticeship program?

The apprenticeship training period is one year (12 months) for all trades. During this period, apprentices will receive practical training at designated railway workshops under the supervision of experienced professionals.

4. Is there any guarantee of employment after apprenticeship?

Successful completion of apprenticeship does not guarantee employment with Indian Railways. However, apprentices receive a National Apprenticeship Certificate which provides them with better employment opportunities in both public and private sectors. Former apprentices also get age relaxation and preference in future railway recruitments.

5. What documents are required during document verification?

You will need to present the following original documents with two sets of photocopies:

  • Matriculation (Class 10) certificate and mark sheet
  • ITI certificate and mark sheet
  • Caste certificate (if applicable)
  • Domicile certificate
  • Photo ID proof (Aadhaar Card/Voter ID)
  • Passport size photographs
  • Application printout with registration number

6. Can final year ITI students apply?

No, only candidates who have completed their ITI course and possess the final certificate are eligible to apply. Provisional certificates are not accepted for this recruitment.

7. Is hostel accommodation provided during apprenticeship?

No, hostel accommodation is not provided by the railway. Apprentices need to make their own arrangements for accommodation during the training period. However, some workshops may have limited facilities which can be availed on payment basis.

8. How does the stipend payment work?

The monthly stipend is paid through electronic transfer to the apprentice's bank account. Payments are usually made by the 10th of the following month. Apprentices must maintain an active bank account and submit their account details during joining.

9. Can I apply for multiple trades?

No, each candidate can apply for only one trade in one division/workshop. Multiple applications will lead to rejection of all applications.

10. What is the working schedule during apprenticeship?

Apprentices follow regular workshop timings, typically 8 hours per day for 6 days a week. The schedule includes both theoretical classes and practical training sessions. Apprentices are entitled to weekly offs and declared holidays as per railway rules.
