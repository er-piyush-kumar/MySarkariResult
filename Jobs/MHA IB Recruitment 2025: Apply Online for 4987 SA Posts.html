About Ministry of Home Affairs Intelligence Bureau

What is Intelligence Bureau?

The Intelligence Bureau (IB) is India's internal intelligence agency. Operating under the Ministry of Home Affairs, IB is responsible for gathering intelligence within the country and counter-intelligence operations. Established in 1887, it is one of the oldest intelligence organizations in the world.

Role of Security Assistant/Executive

Security Assistants/Executives play a crucial role in national security. Key responsibilities include:

  • Gathering and analyzing intelligence information
  • Maintaining security at sensitive installations
  • Conducting background checks and verifications
  • Assisting in counter-intelligence operations
  • Handling confidential documents and information
  • Coordinating with other security agencies
  • Maintaining vigilance against threats to national security

Career Insight:

Working in Intelligence Bureau offers a prestigious career with opportunities for professional growth, specialized training, and serving the nation. IB personnel receive excellent benefits and job security with opportunities for advancement to higher positions.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Is there any reservation for women candidates in MHA IB recruitment?

Yes, MHA IB provides 30% horizontal reservation for women candidates across all categories. This means 30% of seats in each category (General, OBC, SC, ST) are reserved for women applicants.

2. Can candidates from other states apply?

Candidates must apply against the state for which they hold a valid domicile certificate. You can only apply for the state where you have permanent residency as per your domicile certificate.

3. What documents are required during document verification?

Selected candidates need to present:

  • Original 10th mark sheet and certificate
  • Domicile certificate
  • Category certificate (if applicable)
  • Photo identity proof (Aadhaar, Voter ID, etc.)
  • Passport size photographs
  • Character certificate

4. Is typing skill required for Security Assistant/Executive?

Basic computer knowledge is preferred but typing skills are not specifically required for the initial selection. However, selected candidates may receive training in computer operations during their service.

5. What are the career growth opportunities?

Security Assistants/Executives can advance to:

  • Senior Security Assistant
  • Security Executive
  • Assistant Central Intelligence Officer
  • Deputy Central Intelligence Officer
  • Through departmental exams to higher positions

6. Is there any bond period after selection?

Selected candidates are required to serve a minimum period of 5 years in the Intelligence Bureau. Early resignation may require repayment of training costs.

7. What is the training period for selected candidates?

Selected candidates undergo a mandatory training program of 6-9 months at IB training centers. The training includes:

  • Basic intelligence gathering techniques
  • Security procedures
  • Physical fitness training
  • Field craft and observation skills
  • Confidentiality and ethics

8. Can candidates with tattoos apply?

Tattoos are permitted as long as they are not:

  • On the face, neck or forearms
  • Objectionable in nature (religious, political, offensive)
  • Visible when wearing uniform
A medical certificate may be required for tattoo verification.

9. Are there any postings outside the domicile state?

While initial posting is generally in the domicile state, candidates may be transferred to any part of India based on operational requirements of the Intelligence Bureau.

10. What is the probation period?

The probation period for Security Assistant/Executive is typically 2 years. During this period, candidates undergo training and their performance is evaluated. Confirmation in service is subject to satisfactory completion of probation.
